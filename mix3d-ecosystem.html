Overview

A platform is only as coherent as its seams.

Mix3D isn't one app — it's an ecosystem. A learner runs simulations and courses. An enterprise manages teams, licensing and access. A content owner authors and owns content. An instructor assigns, guides and tracks cohorts. Each gets their own admin panel, yet all of it has to resolve into one connected workflow — and one website.

My focus was the architecture and the seams: shared navigation logic, consistent admin patterns, and a single source of truth for how content and permissions flow between roles, so the platform never feels like four products bolted together.

The challenge

Four mental models, one workflow that can't fork.

Each role thinks about the product differently — a content owner cares about ownership and reuse, an enterprise about access and governance, an instructor about cohorts and progress, a learner about just running the thing. Designing four admin panels is easy; designing four panels that share one workflow, one content model and one visual system is the real work.

The risk in any multi-role platform is drift — every role accreting its own patterns until the product fragments. The architecture had to make consistency the path of least resistance.
