Overview

The hard part of AI isn't the model — it's the trust.

Gameplay.ai analyses matches and surfaces behaviour signals and recommendations. The product question wasn't "can we compute it?" — it was "will a player believe it, and act on it?" An insight that can't explain itself gets ignored.

I designed the interface layer that makes AI output actionable: insight framed as plain advice, evidence attached to every claim, and a confidence read-out so users know how much weight to give it. The goal was a product that informs rather than overwhelms.

The challenge

Signal without overwhelm.

Performance data is dense and easy to over-serve. Show everything and you bury the one thing that matters; show too little and the product feels shallow. The design had to rank and frame — lead with the recommendation, support it with the evidence, and let the curious dig deeper without forcing it on everyone.

And because it's AI, every output had to carry its own credibility: what is this based on, and how sure are we? That transparency is what turns a number into a decision.
