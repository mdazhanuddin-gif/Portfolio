Overview

Operations software lives or dies on density.

A fleet operator's screen is never calm — vehicles moving, jobs queuing, exceptions firing, all in real time. FleetDrive 360 had to hold high-density operational data and still let someone act fast and correctly. That's a different discipline from consumer UX: speed, legibility and trust under load matter more than delight.

I owned the information architecture and the operational interfaces — structuring dispatch, live monitoring, maintenance and reporting so each screen answers its question at a glance and the next action is always obvious.

The challenge

Make a flood of live data feel calm.

Everything in fleet ops competes for attention — and an operator can't afford to hunt. The challenge was hierarchy under pressure: surface the exceptions that need action now, keep the rest available but quiet, and design read-outs that stay legible when dozens of vehicles update at once.

Across dispatch, monitoring, maintenance and reporting, the same data had to be reframed for different jobs without fragmenting into four disconnected tools. A coherent IA was the spine that held it together.
