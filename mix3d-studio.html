Overview

A 3D simulation builder anyone can drive.

Mix3D's promise is ambitious: let a writer, instructor or content owner turn an idea into a playable 3D simulation without engineering help. Studio is where that happens — the authoring surface at the centre of a 15-module platform that also spans the Streamer, an LMS, a marketplace, assessments and analytics.

The hard part isn't rendering 3D — it's making authoring feel obvious. A creator needs to place a scene, sequence it frame by frame, and define what reacts to what, all in a language that never asks them to think like a programmer. My job was to design that language and the flows around it: from a blank scene to a published, streamable simulation.

The challenge

Three audiences, one authoring model, zero code.

The same builder had to serve a storyteller shaping narrative beats, an instructor assembling a training scenario, and a content owner producing at scale — each with different mental models and tolerance for complexity. Power users wanted control; first-timers needed a floor they couldn't fall through.

On top of that, simulations are reactive: objects respond to triggers, states change, sequences branch. Expressing that logic visually — clearly enough to author, debug and trust — was the central design problem, and the reason Gameplay.ai's 3D A|R (action–reaction) assist became part of the flow.
