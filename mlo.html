Overview

Operations get complex one exception at a time.

Operational software accumulates complexity — every edge case adds a step, every step adds a place to get stuck. MLO's job was to reduce that complexity through structure: clear approval flows, visible process state, and a management layer that gives oversight without micromanagement.

I designed the UX architecture — how processes are modelled, how approvals move, and how the whole thing stays visible — so the product feels like a system you can reason about rather than a maze you memorise.

The challenge

Make multi-step processes legible.

Approval-driven operations have a visibility problem: requests stall in places no one can see, and no one's sure who's holding what. The design challenge was to make process state obvious — where something is, what's next, and who owns the next move — without burying users in workflow plumbing.

It also had to scale across different processes without each one becoming a bespoke screen. A consistent structure for flows and approvals was the answer.
